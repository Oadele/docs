# Annual Security Awareness Training Program

A practical, repeatable framework for designing and running a year-long security awareness training program that drives real behavior change—not just compliance.

---

## Purpose

This repository provides a **12-month security awareness training template** that organizations can reuse year over year. It balances leadership engagement, short training modules, simulations, and culture-building activities to reduce human risk.

### Designed for:
- Security & GRC teams  
- CISOs and Security Leaders  
- Small to mid-sized organizations  
- Compliance-driven and security-mature environments  

---

## Program Goals

- Build a strong security-first culture  
- Reduce phishing and social engineering risk  
- Improve employee reporting behavior  
- Provide measurable outcomes for leadership  

---

## Core Design Principles

- Leadership sets the tone  
- Training is frequent, short, and relevant  
- Simulations reinforce learning  
- Metrics drive continuous improvement  
- New hires receive security training year-round  

---

## Annual Training Schedule (Template)

### Leadership Kickoff

**Objective:**  
Establish cybersecurity as a shared responsibility.

**Activities:**
- Message from executive leadership or CISO
- Communicate:
  - Why cybersecurity matters
  - Employee role in protecting the organization
  - Overview of the year’s training program

**Deliverable:**
- Email or short video message

---

### Training Module: Social Engineering Defense

**Focus:** Voice-based attacks (Vishing)

**Objective:**
- Identify social engineering tactics
- Recognize red flags in phone-based attacks

**Format:**
- 10–20 minute interactive training
- Scenario-based examples

---

### Cybersecurity Update: Phishing Awareness

**Theme:** Can You Spot a Phishing Email?

**Objective:**
- Reinforce phishing detection skills
- Encourage reporting suspicious emails

**Format:**
- Short email, intranet post, or chat message

---

### Phishing Simulations (Recurring)

**Objective:**
- Measure real-world behavior
- Reinforce training through practice

**Best Practices:**
- Run multiple simulations per year
- Vary difficulty and themes
- Provide feedback and micro-training

**Metrics:**
- Click rate
- Report rate
- Repeat failures

---

### Training Module: Protecting Credentials

**Focus:**
- Password hygiene
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

**Objective:**
- Reduce credential compromise risk
- Explain the importance of MFA

---

### Cybersecurity Update: Strong Passwords

**Objective:**
- Reinforce secure password practices
- Promote password managers (if available)

---

### Cybersecurity Culture Update

**Theme:** Building a Secure Culture Together

**Objective:**
- Reinforce shared responsibility
- Encourage speaking up and reporting issues

---

### Training Module: Malware Defense

**Theme:** Malware Mayhem – Defending Against Digital Invaders

**Objective:**
- Recognize malware delivery methods
- Understand risks from downloads and attachments

---

### Cybersecurity Awareness Month Focus

**Objective:**
- Re-energize security awareness
- Highlight a current threat or risk

**Optional Enhancements:**
- Guest speaker
- Short quiz or challenge

---

### Interactive Training Activity

**Format:**
- Cybersecurity game or competition

**Objective:**
- Increase engagement
- Reinforce learning in a low-pressure way

---

### Year-End Reinforcement

**Objective:**
- Measure improvement
- Identify gaps for next year

**Activities:**
- Final phishing simulation
- Annual metrics review

---

## New Hire Security Training (All Year)

**Objective:**  
Ensure no employee starts without security basics.

**Include in onboarding:**
- Phishing awareness
- Password & MFA training
- Incident and phishing reporting process

**Best Practice:**  
Complete within the first 30 days.
